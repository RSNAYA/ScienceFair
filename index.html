Password Security

Staying safe in an online environment

Introduction

We live in an online digital world. Passwords are the first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts, computer and your personal information. Identity thefts and hacks are on the rise. The stronger your password, the more secure your computer and personal information is. You should maintain strong passwords for all accounts on your computer.

How can you stay safe online?

The most important method to safeguard your personal information is to never use the same password, always use a different password for a different website. If a hacker gains access to the one password you always use, all your personal information can be accessed in a matter of seconds. I have listed other ways to keep your personal information secure below.

  • Your password needs to be at least 8 characters long. There should be a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, symbols, and numbers.
  • Make sure no one is watching when you enter your password for a website.
  • Always log off / sign out of your account if you are leaving your device unattended.
  • Avoid entering passwords over a public wifi such as airport wifi, coffee shops, and libraries. The passwords can be stolen over the wifi connection since it's unsecured.
  • Never share your passwords with anyone.
  • Never allow your browser to save your passwords, instead save them with a password manager.
  • Change your passwords regularly and avoid using the same passwords repeatedly.

Doing this will secure your passwords, but it might be a hassle to always remember to follow the steps, and ensure that your passwords are safe and usable.

Statistics

According to the FTC, the number of stolen identities rose by 45% in 2020, causing financial losses for many people. This was around the time covid was a known threat to the public, and forced many people to go digital.

Due to the rising Coronavirus pandemic, cybercrimes are up by 600%. Many scams such as phishing websites, fake customer support scams, and viruses have taken place through emails and other forms of communication. In 2009, around 12.5 million people were infected with malware. In 2018, that number rose to 812.6 million people. As the world moves into a digital era, we must take the steps needed to protect ourselves.

My Project

This is where my science fair project comes into play. It consists of many tools, made in Python to secure your online presence. It consists of

  • A Password Generator. This tool generates passwords with symbols, a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, and makes them your requested length.
  • A Password Leak Checker. This tool checks your password against multiple databases, which contain leaked passwords. This tells you if your password has been compromised.
  • A Password Checker. This tool tells you if your password is secure, and if it's not, gives you tips on how to make it more secure.
  • A Virus Checker. This checks a file or a url to tell you if it contains malware. If it does, it tells you what type of virus. Using it will ensure that you're safe online, and you don't have malware on your computer.
